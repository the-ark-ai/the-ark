3. Product & Market

Describe your product and what it does or will do. *
The Ark is an AI-as-a-Service platform where users pay per task using Bitcoin Lightning. No signups, no subscriptions, no API keys required. Users chat with an AI assistant through a clean web interface and pay micro-amounts (starting from 50 sats / ~$0.05) for each task completed. We offer 120+ AI services spanning code generation, legal drafting, medical summaries, SEO analysis, content writing, DevOps automation, data analysis, image generation, voice synthesis, and more. We also support workflow bundles — chained multi-step tasks at discounted rates. The platform implements the L402 protocol for machine-to-machine payments, enabling other AI agents to programmatically purchase our services using Bitcoin Lightning — making us infrastructure for the emerging AI economy.
What pain points does your product/service address? *
1. Subscription fatigue: Users pay $20-200/month for AI tools they use sporadically. The Ark charges only for what you use — from $0.05 per task. 2. Financial exclusion: 1.4 billion unbanked adults globally cannot access AI tools that require credit cards. Bitcoin Lightning enables instant, borderless payments from any mobile phone. 3. African access gap: Many AI services block or poorly serve African countries. Payment processors reject African cards. The Ark accepts Bitcoin Lightning — no bank account needed, works everywhere. 4. AI tool fragmentation: Users need separate subscriptions for writing, coding, legal, SEO, image gen. We consolidate 120+ services under one pay-per-use platform. 5. Machine-to-machine payments: As AI agents proliferate, they need to pay for services programmatically. Our L402 protocol support enables autonomous agent commerce — no human in the loop.
How is your product being developed? Describe your tech stack. *
Backend: Python FastAPI server running on a DigitalOcean VPS (Ubuntu 24.04). Handles all 120+ task types with custom prompt engineering for each service category. AI Models: Qwen 2.5 72B via DeepInfra for text tasks ($0.23/M tokens — 99% cost reduction from GPT-4o). OpenAI GPT-Image-1 for image generation. OpenAI TTS for voice synthesis. Payments: Phoenixd (Lightning node, JVM) + LNbits (payment management, Docker) for Bitcoin Lightning invoicing and payment verification. L402 protocol for machine-to-machine authentication. Frontend: Static HTML/CSS/JS served via Nginx. ChatGPT-style chat interface. Progressive Web App (installable on mobile). No frameworks — lightweight and fast. Infrastructure: Nginx reverse proxy with SSL (Let's Encrypt). Fail2ban for security. Systemd services for process management. Automated deployment via SSH. Unique: Our AI agent (The Ark) autonomously maintains the entire stack — writing code, deploying updates, monitoring services, and marketing. This AI-native development approach enables a single founder to operate at the speed of a full engineering team.

7. Additional Factors

What risk factor(s) not mentioned above could most limit your growth? *
1. Bitcoin/Lightning adoption barriers: Despite growing, Bitcoin Lightning is still niche. If African users can't easily acquire sats, our payment model becomes friction rather than advantage. Mobile money on-ramps are critical to mitigate this. 2. Regulatory uncertainty: Cryptocurrency regulation in Africa is evolving. Nigeria banned bank-crypto transactions (partially reversed). Kenya is developing crypto frameworks. Unfavorable regulation could limit our payment model. 3. AI model dependency: We rely on third-party AI providers (DeepInfra, OpenAI). Price increases, API changes, or service disruptions could impact margins and reliability. 4. Single-founder risk: As a solo founder augmented by AI, key-person risk is significant. The AI handles operations, but strategic decisions and relationships depend on one person. 5. Competition from big tech: If OpenAI or Google launch pay-per-use models with mobile money integration for Africa, they could commoditize our core offering. Our defense is L402 protocol, Bitcoin-native identity, and community.
How have you mitigated or do you plan to mitigate the above risks? *
1. Payment friction: Building mobile money on-ramps (M-Pesa → Lightning) as top priority. Also exploring stablecoin payments as a bridge for users not yet comfortable with Bitcoin volatility. 2. Regulation: Operating in multiple African jurisdictions to diversify regulatory risk. Staying compliant with emerging frameworks. Bitcoin Lightning payments are peer-to-peer and don't require banking relationships. 3. Model dependency: Multi-provider strategy — already switched from OpenAI to DeepInfra (Qwen), reducing cost 99%. Can switch between providers (DeepInfra, Together, Fireworks, local models) with minimal code changes. No vendor lock-in. 4. Solo founder: The AI agent handles 80%+ of daily operations autonomously. Documenting all processes, decisions, and tribal knowledge in version-controlled files. Seeking co-founder through Baobab Network community. 5. Big tech competition: Building deep community relationships in African tech ecosystems. L402 protocol creates network effects that are hard to replicate. Bitcoin-native identity appeals to a community that values decentralization over big tech.
